// background & history

what do you need to know about

...second life?

Second Life is an online virtual world that was launched by Linden Lab in 2003. Any registered user can represent themselves through a virtual avatar while also exploring the world socializing with other avatars and even interacting with the environment by building. Your virtual avatar is able to participate in task-based games and can modify the world around you to fit whatever vision you might have.

The Founder, Philip Rosedale, initially formed Linden Lab with the intentions of creating computer hardware that would allow you to be immersed into a virtual world, but eventually that idea changed into a software application of Second Life. The earliest software prototype that was engineered was known as “The Rig” and was worn on your shoulders to help immerse you into the environment you were interacting with.

By 2005, Second Life garnered a lot of media attention and saw an exponential growth in its regular user base with an estimation of 600,000 regularly active accounts.

source: Linden Lab. Linden Research, Inc., 2017.

...immersive environments?

As early the late 1950s, Morton Heilig introduced immersive environments to the general public. Heilig created the first multisensory virtual experience in a product called the Sensorama that diminished sensory input from the world around the user. It enclosed the user and replaced their senses with different media that included objects such as film, sound, vibration, wind, or even odor. Before computers were even invented, VR type technologies were already in existence. It is evident humans already had an innate curiosity before the creation of modern day technology to experience things in new ways in different types of realities.

In one of the first virtual worlds, Worlds Chat, new users did not yet understand the social norms for these kinds of simulations (such as, is it okay to walk through another avatar). Due to this never before seen technology, many users felt like they had to follow the social norms of the real world because they didn’t know anything different. However, the communities of virtual worlds would develop their own social norms, determining what is acceptable behavior and what is not.

Today, immersive environments are all around you, whether it’s an online application accessed through your browser, an application you play through your gaming console, or through the use of a virtual reality headset, humans are immersed into other environments that are not their own on a daily basis.

source: Grimshaw, Mark. The Oxford Handbook of Virtuality. Oxford University Press, 2014.

// the story

...what are we doing?

It has been a few weeks since Gayle’s arrival and the residents of Social Island have began to leave for expeditions. Some residents decided to explore the skies and are building an island somewhere in the unexplored atmosphere. A handful of residents are exploring the land of Penguins, Minecraft, Space, and virtual reality. However, a few residents, including our team, have decided to stick around Second Life’s Social Island.

One day while everyone was up and roaming around the island, Gayle decided to go scuba diving and was unfortunately engulfed by the lagoon (at least, that’s what we think). The same day, the SL 2.0 family decided to go find Gayle and invite her to dinner… but she was nowhere to be found. Looking around for Gayle, they happen upon a sign, indicating that all of the Social Island residents must immediately return home in order to conduct a island wide search for Gayle.

The SL 2.0 team immediately reached out to all residents who are currently on their expeditions to return home at once to Social Island to help rescue Gayle from her deathly peril. It seems like she’s found her way to the afterlife and it’s our responsibility to bring her back to life.

But first, how are we going to get there? Where is Gayle exactly? Is her soul wandering around lost... in the underworld? What do the residents have to do to bring her back to life?

It is up to the other residents to gather together in search of the access point to the underworld and figure out exactly how they will be able to enter it. They must then complete an extensive search within the afterlife for her lost soul by following clues (...there’s a myth about a small, underworld dragon that might be able to lend some hints…) so they can bring her home.

the rules

Find the entire list of detailed rules here and what actions will get you eliminated from completing our tasks!

task one

find the portal

Time to complete: 5 minutes.

Each team can take a look at the sign, located by the town center near the lagoon, that was left behind by an anonymous figure about Gayle’s disappearance. Then, every team must find the portal that is located somewhere around the Social Island to teleport to the outside of the alternate world.

Any team member from their team who finds the portal must either shout their team name in chat that they found the portal or voice it over in Sococo.

Reward: the first team who finds the portal will get a small clue for the second task.

task two

teleport to the alternate world

Time to complete: 5 minutes.

Each team must solve how to enter the alternate world after being teleported to right outside of the alternate realm as an avatar cannot simply enter the realm as usual. Once each team has a solution, they must choose a member to successfully perform the solution to enter the afterlife.

Any team member from their team who enters the realm must either shout their team name in chat that they got into the alternate world or voice it over in Sococo.

Reward: the first team who finds the solution will get a small clue for the last task.

task three

find the three horcruxes

Time to complete: 10 minutes.

Each team must work together and find the three different horcruxes that are hidden around the alternate realm in the structures that we have built. Keep in mind, each one of these horcruxes aren't an easy find! Think of every possible hiding place you can think of and look there! Who knows, there's a rumor a dragon may be able to provide you with some hints.

After finding the three horcruxes, the team must find the Dragon AI and the alternate world Goddess to submit their horcruxes.

Reward: the first team to submit all three horcruxes gets 10 extra class points.
