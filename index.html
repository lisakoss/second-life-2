// background & history

what do you need to know about

...second life?

Second Life is an online virtual world that was launched by Linden Lab in 2003. Any registered user can represent themselves through a virtual avatar while also exploring the world socializing with other avatars and even interacting with the environment by building. Your virtual avatar is able to participate in task-based games and can modify the world around you to fit whatever vision you might have.

The Founder, Philip Rosedale, initially formed Linden Lab with the intentions of creating computer hardware that would allow you to be immersed into a virtual world, but eventually that idea changed into a software application of Second Life. The earliest software prototype that was engineered was known as “The Rig” and was worn on your shoulders to help immerse you into the environment you were interacting with.

By 2005, Second Life garnered a lot of media attention and saw an exponential growth in its regular user base with an estimation of 600,000 regularly active accounts.

source: Linden Lab. Linden Research, Inc., 2017.

...immersive environments?

As early the late 1950s, Morton Heilig introduced immersive environments to the general public. Heilig created the first multisensory virtual experience in a product called the Sensorama that diminished sensory input from the world around the user. It enclosed the user and replaced their senses with different media that included objects such as film, sound, vibration, wind, or even odor. Before computers were even invented, VR type technologies were already in existence. It is evident humans already had an innate curiosity before the creation of modern day technology to experience things in new ways in different types of realities.

In one of the first virtual worlds, Worlds Chat, new users did not yet understand the social norms for these kinds of simulations (such as, is it okay to walk through another avatar). Due to this never before seen technology, many users felt like they had to follow the social norms of the real world because they didn’t know anything different. However, the communities of virtual worlds would develop their own social norms, determining what is acceptable behavior and what is not.

Today, immersive environments are all around you, whether it’s an online application accessed through your browser, an application you play through your gaming console, or through the use of a virtual reality headset, humans are immersed into other environments that are not their own on a daily basis.

source: Grimshaw, Mark. The Oxford Handbook of Virtuality. Oxford University Press, 2014.

branding

Our color for our website include shades of a light blue along with shades of grays which are also represented in our world of Second Life. We followed the color scheme given to us (blue/gray), which worked well for our dark, somewhat scary theme representing Tim Burton’s style of films. Our branded image, which represents a dark version of our once bright Social Island, is represented throughout both our website and 3D virtual world. We have taken various shades of dark grays and blacks represented in our website and found structures, decorations, and a ground color that incorporated blacks, grays, and blues in them along with choosing to build a dark, run down tree house (which represents Ran’s house on Social Island) to help tie our dark and terrifying theme of the afterlife together with the original Social Island.

// the story

...what are we doing?

It has been a few weeks since Gayle’s arrival and the residents of Social Island have began to leave for expeditions. Some residents decided to explore the skies and are building an island somewhere in the unexplored atmosphere. A handful of residents are exploring the land of Penguins, Minecraft, Space, and virtual reality. However, a few residents, including our team, have decided to stick around Second Life’s Social Island.

One day while everyone was up and roaming around the island, Gayle decided to go scuba diving and was unfortunately engulfed by the lagoon (at least, that’s what we think). The same day, the SL 2.0 family decided to go find Gayle and invite her to dinner… but she was nowhere to be found. Looking around for Gayle, they happen upon a sign, indicating that all of the Social Island residents must immediately return home in order to conduct a island wide search for Gayle.

The SL 2.0 team immediately reached out to all residents who are currently on their expeditions to return home at once to Social Island to help rescue Gayle from her deathly peril. It seems like she’s found her way to the afterlife and it’s our responsibility to bring her back to life.

But first, how are we going to get there? Where is Gayle exactly? Is her soul wandering around lost... in the underworld? What do the residents have to do to bring her back to life?

It is up to the other residents to gather together in search of the access point to the underworld and figure out exactly how they will be able to enter it. They must then complete an extensive search within the afterlife for her lost soul by following clues (...there’s a myth about a small, underworld dragon that might be able to lend some hints…) so they can bring her home.

// the tasks

...what's our plan?

As a team, our objective is to modify the current INFO 466 island on Second Life. To do this, we have proposed to introduce our new changes within a Skybox as a way to minimize the loading times experienced due to all of the content everyone has already added to the island. Within our Skybox, we are introducing a new theme of Tim Burton as well to create a sense of the 'afterlife' that goes along with the topic of our story in the search of Gayle.

We also added artificial intelligence to our skybox in the form of a dragon. This dragon will help teams complete our last task by providing hints when necessary with the help of our dragon tamer. For example, this dragon will give out the horcrux locations when teams interact with it by typing certain keywords so they can find the items faster during our last task.

Our team's slack channel is where we keep track of each team member's weekly task and their current progress on completing it. Since our objectives change each week based on the current homework assignment, each team member is assigned a weekly task. We split up the tasks among our team members according to their skills and set appropriate deadlines to ensure that all necessary steps are taken to complete the needed objectives each week.

Check under weekly updates to view our current collaborative homework document. It includes all of the information we are working on for the week that will be submitted at the assignment's deadline.

the rules

Find the entire list of detailed rules here and what actions will get you eliminated from completing our tasks.

task one

find the portal

Time to complete: 2 minutes.

Before starting this task, each team will be assigned to a Patronus who will act as their group leader, guiding them through the first two tasks. Two teams will be following a single Patronus and will need to pay close attention to their instructions. During this task, teams will follow their Patronus to the door that is under the Lagoon. This is the location where Gayle drowned that will teleport them to outside our alternate world in search of her soul.

As soon as a team has found the teleport and have teleported to our alternate world under the lagoon, someone from their team needs to type into Sococo chat the following:

your team’s abbreviated name “teleported.”

Reward: the first team who types in Sococo that they found the portal will earn $100 L for each team member.

task two

teleport to the alternate world

Time to complete: 3 minutes.

Once outside of our alternate world, two team members from each team must enter the skybox without using coordinates to get inside by following the instructions given to them by their Patronus (or by looking at the task two instructions on top of the skybox). After inside the skybox, these two team members need to head to their Patronus and drop a SLURL to their other team members in Sococo so they can also meet up with the Patronus.

When a team has successfully entered the skybox and found their Patronus at their designated location, someone from their team needs to type into Sococo chat the following:

your team’s abbreviated name “patronus.”

Reward: the first team who types in Sococo that they found the portal will earn $100 L for each team member.

task three

find the three horcruxes

Time to complete: 10 minutes.

Each team must work together and find the three different horcruxes that are hidden around the alternate realm. There are genuine and false horcruxes hidden around the skybox, but the real ones are buyable for $0 L. The AI Dragon and the two clue givers will also help teams by giving them clues about the locations of the horcruxes.

After finding the three horcruxes, bring them to the Goddess (item collector) located in the Circus Building. All three horcruxes must be placed on the table where the Goddess is located and all team members must be present. When submitting the horcuxes, someone from their team needs to type into Sococo chat the following:

your team’s abbreviated name “horcruxes.”

Reward: the first team to submit all three horcruxes will receive 10 extra class points per team member and will bring Gayle back to life.
